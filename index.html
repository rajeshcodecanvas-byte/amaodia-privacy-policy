Privacy Policy

Effective Date: April 10, 2026

Welcome to AmaOdia. We respect your privacy and are committed to protecting any data processed by our application. This Privacy Policy outlines our practices regarding user information for the AmaOdia Android App.

Transparency Note: AmaOdia is designed to function primarily offline. We do not collect, upload, or sell your personal data to any third-party servers.

1. Information Handling (Local Storage)

The App stores information locally on your device to provide a personalized experience. This data includes:

  • Search History: Saved locally to allow you to revisit recent translations. You can clear this at any time.
  • Favorites: Words you choose to save for quick access are kept in a local database on your phone.

2. Mandatory Permissions & Their Usage

To function correctly as a modern dictionary and translator, the App requires specific Android permissions:

  • Clipboard Access: Our Floating Widget monitors the system clipboard to detect copied words for instant translation. This text is processed entirely on-device. We never transmit clipboard contents over the internet.
  • Overlay Permission (Display Over Other Apps): This allows the App to show the translation bubble while you are using other applications.
  • Notification Permission: Required to deliver the "Word of the Day" updates and service status notifications.

3. Third-Party Services

We use trusted third-party SDKs that may collect information used to identify you, governed by their own policies:

  • Google Play Services (For updates and licensing)
  • Google Text-to-Speech (For word pronunciation)

4. Security

We value your trust. We use standard encryption and code obfuscation (R8/ProGuard) to protect the App’s internal logic. Please ensure your mobile device is secured with a screen lock to protect the local database stored on your hardware.

5. Children’s Privacy

AmaOdia does not knowingly collect any personally identifiable information from children under the age of 13. If you are a parent and believe your child has provided data, please contact us.
